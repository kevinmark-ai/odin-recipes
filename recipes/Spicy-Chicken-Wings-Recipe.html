Spicy Chicken Wings

Description

There are ingredients that are stellar in many preparations, without them it would be impossible to achieve wonderful dishes. But with respect to chicken, without a doubt, the delicious recipe for spicy chicken wings is more than just another way of cooking, you know why.

the spicy chicken wings are in our lives forever. When going to a restaurant, or spending a different Sunday at home watching a football game or while spending an afternoon watching a movie on television. Surely you’ve seen them in a lot of advertising images and, often, just by imagining them, your appetite opens up.

Ingredients

  • 6 tablespoons Tabasco sauce (240 mL).
  • 1 and 1/2 cups of butter (125 g).
  • 10 cups with chicken wings (1 kg).
  • 1 spoonful of red cayenne pepper (25 g).
  • 3 cups of red tomato puree (360 mL).
  • 1 spoonful of curry powder (25 g).
  • (1) teaspoon red chili powder (25 g).

Steps

  1. The first thing you should do is remove the excess skin from all the chicken wings. So that the largest amount of meat is available.
  2. Then, preheat the oven to a temperature of 190 ° C.
  3. Then, without having seasoned the wings, take them to the oven for 15 minutes and wait until they are well cooked and browned. Keep in mind that the goal of using the oven instead of frying the wings.
  4. Now, take a medium bowl and start with the preparation of the hot sauce.
  5. For this, you will use the butter, Tabasco sauce, the spoonful of chili, the red cayenne pepper, and the curry.
  6. You must make sure you mix all the ingredients very well. The best advice you can follow is to go slowly trying your mix and try not to overdo salty or too spicy. Try to balance it.
  7. Once the chicken wings are removed from the oven and warm, proceed to submerge them in your hot sauce preparation and soak each one generously.
  8. Place all the wings, already bathed in the sauce, back to the oven for another 15 minutes. After a while, retire and simply enjoy everything with great pleasure from each bite.
